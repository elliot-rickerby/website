elliot rickerby is a freelance cultural researcher and psychologist. blending an empirical academic background with a passion for fashion, elliot aims to use quality research and scientific understanding to solve problems within the fashion industry. although specialising in mental health and consumer insights, he believes that every part of the industry stands to gain from a deeper understanding of the behaviours that shape its consumers, workforce and beyond.

elliot has carried out research for Uniqlo as well as a range of independent designers, alongside contributing to several academic papers. his master's thesis - scheduled for publication in 2026 - marks an early step in addressing the mental health pressures within the fashion industry. he also writes regularly, with a collection of published articles and a Substack where he analyses fashion media.

elliot is open to collaborations and freelance work and is best contacted via the contact page, LinkedIn or Instagram.

uniqlo consumer insights

detailed research on consumer behavior patterns for uniqlo's european market expansion. conducted qualitative interviews and quantitative surveys across three countries.

methods: mixed-methods, survey design, thematic analysis

fashion industry mental health

master's thesis research exploring mental health pressures in fashion design education. longitudinal study with 120 participants across two academic years.

methods: longitudinal study, psychometric assessment, interview analysis

sustainable consumption patterns

analysis of behavioral barriers to sustainable fashion consumption. research informed a startup's product development strategy.

methods: behavioral economics framework, focus groups, a/b testing

independent designer branding

brand positioning research for emerging independent fashion labels. developed psychographic profiles for target audiences.

methods: competitor analysis, brand audit, consumer profiling
